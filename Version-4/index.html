01 / The obligation

A product launch creates obligations that last for years.

An insurer can stop selling a product that does not perform as expected. It must still support the policies already issued against it.

The carrier must process endorsements and renewals, handle claims under the coverage sold, maintain its rates and forms, and keep the supporting systems operational.

Regulators protect policyholders from abrupt changes to their coverage. Customers expect the insurer to honor the contract it sold.

Each launch creates a legal, regulatory, and technology commitment that continues after the launch team has moved on.

05 / The platform

The platform structures the product itself.

The Oversee platform maintains a carrier-owned definition of the rates, rules, coverages, forms, classifications, and variations that define an insurance product. The platform generates the specifications, configuration, mappings, and tests required by each target system.

The product definition remains independent of any one core platform or implementation partner. The carrier retains its repositories, engineering controls, testing processes, and release gates.

Downstream systems and AI

Underwriting, servicing, claims, and engineering AI all need to know which product version applies, which rules govern it, and where the requirements came from. The platform gives those systems a structured product model they can reference instead of requiring each one to infer the product from documents, platform configuration, and institutional knowledge.
