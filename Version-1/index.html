01 / The obligation

A product launch creates obligations that last for years.

An insurer can stop selling a product that does not perform as expected. It must still support the policies already issued against it.

The carrier must process endorsements and renewals, handle claims under the coverage sold, maintain filed rates and forms, and keep the supporting systems operational.

Regulators protect policyholders from abrupt changes to their coverage. Customers expect the insurer to honour the contract it sold.

Each launch creates a legal, regulatory and technology commitment that continues after the launch team has moved on.
